### Q1. Subject-Verb Agreement (GATE 2024) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Choose the grammatically correct sentence:

(A) Each of the students have completed their assignment.  
(B) Each of the students has completed their assignment.  
(C) Each of the students have completed his assignment.  
(D) Each of the students has completed his or her assignment.

**Solution:**

**Rule:** "Each" is always singular ‚Üí takes singular verb "has"

Also, "their" is acceptable modern usage for gender-neutral singular

**Analysis:**
- (A) ‚ùå "have" is wrong (should be "has")
- (B) ‚úÖ Correct grammar ("has" + "their" modern acceptable)
- (C) ‚ùå "have" is wrong
- (D) ‚úÖ Also correct ("has" + "his or her" traditional)

**Best answer:** **(B)** - more natural modern usage

**Memory:** "Each/Every/Either/Neither ‚Üí Singular verb"

**Question:**

### Q4. Sentence Completion (GATE 2023) ‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Despite _____ resources, the team managed to complete the project on time.

(A) abundant  
(B) limited  
(C) sufficient  
(D) adequate

**Solution:**

**Key word:** "Despite" indicates **contrast**

If project completed on time **despite** something ‚Üí that something must be **negative/challenging**

**Limited resources** = challenge ‚Üí makes "Despite" logical

**Answer: (B) limited**

**Logic:** "Despite limited resources" = contrast makes sense

### Q5. Error Detection (GATE 2023) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Identify the error:

(A) The number of students  
(B) in the class  
(C) are increasing  
(D) every year.

**Solution:**

**Error in (C):** "are increasing"

**Reason:** "The number" is singular ‚Üí needs singular verb "is"

**Correct:** "The number of students in the class **is** increasing every year."

**Note:** "A number of students **are**" (plural) vs "The number **is**" (singular)

**Answer: (C) are increasing**

### Q8. Tense (GATE 2021) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
By next month, I _____ in this company for five years.

(A) am working  
(B) will work  
(C) will have worked  
(D) will be working

**Solution:**

**Time marker:** "By next month" = completion point in future

**Action:** Working for five years = duration until completion point

**Tense needed:** Future Perfect ("will have + V3")

**Answer: (C) will have worked**

**Memory:** "By [future time]" ‚Üí Future Perfect

### Q11. Voice Change (GATE 2020) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Active: "The teacher explained the concept clearly."  
Passive:

(A) The concept was explained clearly by the teacher.  
(B) The concept is explained clearly by the teacher.  
(C) The concept has been explained clearly by the teacher.  
(D) The concept will be explained clearly by the teacher.

**Solution:**

**Active:** Teacher explained (simple past)

**Passive:** was/were + V3 (past participle)

**Structure:** Object + was/were + V3 + by + Subject

**Answer: (A) The concept was explained clearly by the teacher.**

### Q12. Jumbled Sentences (GATE 2019) ‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Arrange in logical order:

P: However, excessive use can be harmful  
Q: Technology has revolutionized our lives  
R: Therefore, balance is essential  
S: It has made communication easier

(A) QSPR  
(B) QPSR  
(C) SQPR  
(D) PQRS

**Solution:**

**Logical flow:**
1. **Q:** Technology has revolutionized our lives (introduction)
2. **S:** It has made communication easier (positive aspect)
3. **P:** However, excessive use can be harmful (contrast)
4. **R:** Therefore, balance is essential (conclusion)

**Answer: (A) QSPR**

**Flow:** Intro ‚Üí Benefit ‚Üí Problem ‚Üí Solution

### Q14. Para Completion (GATE 2018) ‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question:**  
"Artificial intelligence is transforming industries. From healthcare to finance, AI applications are everywhere. _____. However, ethical concerns must be addressed."

Best fit:

(A) AI will replace all human jobs  
(B) Its potential benefits are immense  
(C) Technology is dangerous  
(D) Humans should avoid AI

**Solution:**

**Before blank:** Positive (AI transforming, applications everywhere)

**After blank:** "However" ‚Üí indicates contrast ‚Üí next sentence about concerns

**Blank needs:** Continuation of positive before contrast

**Answer: (B) Its potential benefits are immense**

**Logic:** Positive ‚Üí More positive ‚Üí "However" ‚Üí Concerns

### Q15. Sentence Improvement (GATE 2018) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
"He is **more stronger** than his brother."

(A) more strong  
(B) stronger  
(C) most strong  
(D) No improvement

**Solution:**

**Error:** "more stronger" = double comparative (wrong!)

**Rule:** 
- One-syllable adjectives: strong ‚Üí **stronger** (not "more strong")
- Multi-syllable: beautiful ‚Üí **more beautiful**

**Correct:** "He is **stronger** than his brother."

**Answer: (B) stronger**

**Memory:** Don't use "more" + "er" together!

## üìä Summary

### Grammar Rules Tested
1. **Subject-verb agreement:** Each/Every/Either ‚Üí singular
2. **Number of vs A number of:** The number is / A number are
3. **Tenses:** By [time] ‚Üí Perfect tense
4. **Voice:** Active ‚Üî Passive conversion
5. **Comparatives:** Don't double (not "more stronger")

### Vocabulary Tips
1. Learn common synonyms/antonyms
2. Idioms with meanings
3. One-word substitutions (-phile, -phobe, etc.)
4. Preposition pairs (addicted to, good at)

### Comprehension Strategy
1. Read question first
2. Skim passage for keywords
3. Match with answer choices
4. Eliminate wrong options

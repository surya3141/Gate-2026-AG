## üéØ Introduction

### What is Farm Power?

Farm power is the energy required to operate farm machinery and equipment for various agricultural operations.

### Importance

**Why farm power matters:**
- Increases productivity (5-10√ó compared to manual)
- Reduces drudgery
- Enables timely operations
- Improves crop yields
- Reduces cost of production per unit

---

### Units of Power

**Power = Rate of doing work**

$$
P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{F \times d}{t} = F \times v
$$

**Common Units:**
- **Watt (W):** SI unit, 1 W = 1 J/s
- **Kilowatt (kW):** 1 kW = 1000 W
- **Horsepower (HP):** 1 HP = 0.746 kW = 745.7 W
- **Metric HP (PS):** 1 PS = 0.735 kW

**Conversions:**
$$
1 \text{ HP} = 0.746 \text{ kW} = 746 \text{ W} = 735.5 \text{ W (metric)}
$$

$$
1 \text{ kW} = 1.34 \text{ HP} = 1.36 \text{ PS}
$$

**Memory Trick:** **"3/4 kW per HP"** (0.746 ‚âà 0.75)

## üë§ Human Power

### Human as Power Source

**Capacity:**
- Average adult: 0.05-0.1 HP (37-75 W) sustained
- Peak: Up to 0.5 HP (373 W) for short duration
- Typical sustained work: 60-80 W for 8 hours

**Efficiency:** 15-25% (mechanical efficiency)

---

### Types of Human Work

**1. Manual Work:**
- Digging, sowing, weeding
- Power: 40-60 W sustained

**2. Pedal Power:**
- Bicycle, pedal thresher
- Power: 75-100 W sustained
- More efficient than manual

**3. Hand-operated Tools:**
- Hand hoe, sickle, spade
- Power output: 30-50 W

---

### Advantages and Limitations

**Advantages:**
- ‚úÖ No fuel cost
- ‚úÖ Flexible and intelligent
- ‚úÖ Suitable for small plots
- ‚úÖ No environmental impact

**Limitations:**
- ‚ùå Very low power output
- ‚ùå High drudgery
- ‚ùå Slow operations
- ‚ùå Limited to light work
- ‚ùå Health impacts

**Modern role:** Supervision, skilled operations, small gardens

## üêÑ Animal Power

### Animals as Power Source

**Common draft animals:**
- Bullocks/Oxen
- Buffaloes
- Horses
- Mules
- Camels (arid regions)
- Elephants (forestry)

---

### Power Output

**Bullock (most common in India):**
- **Sustained power:** 0.5-0.75 HP (375-560 W)
- **Peak power:** 1.0 HP for short duration
- **Working hours:** 4-6 hours/day
- **Working speed:** 2-3 km/h
- **Pulling force:** 10-15% of body weight

**Draft calculation:**
For 400 kg bullock:
- Draft force = 400 √ó 0.12 = 48 kg ‚âà 480 N
- At 2.5 km/h = 0.69 m/s
- Power = 480 √ó 0.69 = 331 W = 0.44 HP ‚úì

---

### Factors Affecting Animal Power

**1. Body Weight:**
- Heavier = More power
- Typical: 350-500 kg

**2. Breed:**
- Local breeds: Hardy, less power
- Improved breeds: More power, need better care

**3. Age:**
- Prime: 4-10 years
- Training starts: 2.5-3 years

**4. Health and Nutrition:**
- Well-fed animals: 30-50% more power
- Requires 10-15 kg dry fodder/day

**5. Training:**
- Well-trained: Efficient, less stress
- Training period: 3-6 months

**6. Ambient Conditions:**
- Heat reduces power (25-30% loss in summer)
- Humidity affects performance

---

### Animal-Drawn Implements

**Examples:**
- Wooden/iron plough
- Harrow
- Seed drill
- Bullock cart
- Puddler (rice)

**Power requirement:** 0.3-0.8 HP per implement

---

### Advantages and Limitations

**Advantages:**
- ‚úÖ Multi-purpose (draft, transport, dung, milk)
- ‚úÖ No fuel cost
- ‚úÖ Suitable for small farms
- ‚úÖ By-products (manure)
- ‚úÖ Can work in wet conditions

**Limitations:**
- ‚ùå Low power (0.5-0.75 HP)
- ‚ùå Slow speed (2-3 km/h)
- ‚ùå Limited working hours (4-6 h/day)
- ‚ùå Maintenance cost (feed, health)
- ‚ùå Not suitable for heavy operations

**Current status:** Declining but still used on 40-50% Indian farms

## üöú Mechanical Power

### Tractor - Primary Source

**Definition:** Self-propelled power unit designed for pulling and operating agricultural machinery.

**Power range:**
- Small: 20-35 HP
- Medium: 35-50 HP
- Large: 50-100 HP
- Very large: >100 HP

---

### Types of Tractors

**1. Wheel Tractors:**
- Most common
- 2WD or 4WD
- Versatile

**2. Track/Crawler Tractors:**
- Better traction
- For difficult terrain
- Heavier, slower

**3. Garden Tractors:**
- Small (5-15 HP)
- For horticulture

---

### Power from Tractor

**Three forms:**

**1. Drawbar Power (DBP):**
- For pulling implements
- Available at hitch point
- Typically 60-75% of engine power

**2. PTO Power:**
- For operating machines (thresher, pump)
- At 540 or 1000 rpm
- Typically 80-85% of engine power

**3. Belt Power:**
- Through belt pulley
- Less common now
- For stationary operations

---

### Advantages of Tractors

**Compared to animals:**
- ‚úì 20-50√ó more power
- ‚úì Works 10-12 hours/day
- ‚úì Faster (5-8 km/h field, 20-30 km/h road)
- ‚úì Can operate heavy implements
- ‚úì Multi-purpose (ploughing, transport, PTO)

**Economic:**
- Break-even: >4 hectares typically
- Reduces labor requirement
- Timely operations ‚Üí better yields

---

### Power Tillers

**Definition:** Walking-type tractor with 8-18 HP engine

**Features:**
- Operator walks behind
- Rotavator or wheels
- Lower cost than tractor

**Applications:**
- Puddling (rice)
- Small farms (1-2 ha)
- Hilly areas
- Horticultural crops

**Power:** 8-18 HP (6-13 kW)

---

### Stationary Engines

**Types:**
- Diesel engines: 5-20 HP
- Petrol/kerosene engines: 3-10 HP

**Uses:**
- Pumping irrigation water
- Operating threshers
- Flour mills
- Oil expellers

**Advantages:**
- Lower cost than tractor
- Dedicated for specific operation
- Higher efficiency for stationary work

## üßÆ Important Calculations

### Power Calculation

**From force and velocity:**
$$
P = F \times v
$$

Where:
- P = Power (W)
- F = Force (N)
- v = Velocity (m/s)

**Example:**
Draft force = 2000 N, Speed = 1.5 m/s
$$
P = 2000 \times 1.5 = 3000 \text{ W} = 3 \text{ kW} = 4 \text{ HP}
$$

---

### Human Power Output

**From work done:**
$$
P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{m \times g \times h}{t}
$$

**Example:**
Person lifts 20 kg to 1.5 m height in 2 seconds
$$
P = \frac{20 \times 9.81 \times 1.5}{2} = \frac{294.3}{2} = 147 \text{ W} = 0.2 \text{ HP}
$$

---

### Animal Draft Power

**Draft force = % of body weight**

Typical: 10-15% of body weight

**Power:**
$$
P = \text{Draft} \times \text{Speed}
$$

**Example:**
Bullock: 400 kg, Speed: 2.5 km/h = 0.694 m/s
Draft = 12% of weight = 48 kg = 480 N
$$
P = 480 \times 0.694 = 333 \text{ W} = 0.45 \text{ HP}
$$

---

### Energy Equivalents

**1 liter diesel ‚âà 10 kWh of energy**

For engine with 30% efficiency:
$$
\text{Useful work} = 10 \times 0.30 = 3 \text{ kWh}
$$

**Biogas equivalent:**
$$
1 \text{ m}^3 \text{ biogas} ‚âà 0.6 \text{ L diesel}
$$

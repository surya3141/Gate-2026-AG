## ðŸŽ¯ Key Concepts Summary

### Power Definitions

**Power:** Rate of doing work
$$
P = \frac{W}{t} \text{ (Watts or HP)}
$$

**1 HP = 0.746 kW = 735.5 W**

**Brake Power (BP):** Actual power available at engine shaft
$$
BP = \frac{2\pi NT}{60000} \text{ kW}
$$

**Drawbar Power (DBP):** Power available at drawbar for pulling
$$
DBP = \frac{P \times V}{3.6} \text{ kW}
$$

---

### Tractor Performance

**Tractive Efficiency:**
$$
\eta_t = \frac{DBP}{BP} \times 100\%
$$

**Fuel Consumption:**
$$
\text{SFC (g/kWh)} = \frac{\text{Fuel consumed (g)}}{\text{Power (kW)} \times \text{Time (h)}}
$$

### Standard Tractor Ratings

| Parameter | Small | Medium | Large |
|-----------|-------|--------|-------|
| **Engine power** | 20-35 HP | 35-50 HP | >50 HP |
| **PTO power** | 18-30 HP | 30-45 HP | >45 HP |
| **Weight** | 1200-1800 kg | 1800-2500 kg | >2500 kg |

### PTO Speeds

- **Standard:** 540 rpm
- **High speed:** 1000 rpm
- **Some tractors:** 750 rpm

### Gear Ratios

- **Low gear:** High torque, low speed (ploughing)
- **High gear:** Low torque, high speed (transport)
- **Typical:** 6-12 forward, 2-4 reverse gears

## ðŸ§® Key Formulas

### Engine Performance

**Brake Power:**
$$
BP = \frac{2\pi NT}{60000} \text{ kW}
$$

**Indicated Power:**
$$
IP = \frac{P_{mi} \times L \times A \times n \times N}{60000} \text{ kW}
$$

**Mechanical Efficiency:**
$$
\eta_m = \frac{BP}{IP} \times 100\%
$$

---

### Fuel Calculations

**Specific Fuel Consumption:**
$$
SFC = \frac{m_f}{BP \times t} \text{ (g/kWh)}
$$

**Brake Thermal Efficiency:**
$$
\eta_{bth} = \frac{BP}{\dot{m}_f \times CV} \times 100\%
$$

---

### Traction

**Drawbar Pull:**
$$
P = W \times \mu \times \frac{W_r}{W}
$$

**Rolling Resistance:**
$$
R_r = W \times C_r
$$

**Tractive Efficiency:**
$$
\eta_t = \frac{\text{Pull} \times \text{Speed}}{\text{Engine Power}} \times 100\%
$$

### Q2. aw Definition (GATE 2024) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Water activity is defined as:

(A) Weight of water / Total weight  
(B) Vapor pressure of food / Vapor pressure of pure water

(D) Moisture content percentage

**Solution:**

**Water activity (aw):**
$$

$$

where:
- P = Vapor pressure of food
- P‚ÇÄ = Vapor pressure of pure water at same temperature
- ERH = Equilibrium Relative Humidity (%)

**Range:** 0 to 1.0 (pure water = 1.0)

**Answer: (B) Vapor pressure of food / Vapor pressure of pure water**

### Q3. Freezing Rate Effect (GATE 2023) ‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Fast freezing compared to slow freezing produces:

(A) Large ice crystals  
(B) Small ice crystals  
(C) No ice crystals  
(D) Same size crystals

**Solution:**

**Freezing rate effects:**

| Freezing Type | Crystal Size | Quality | Cell Damage |
|---------------|--------------|---------|-------------|
| **Fast** | **Small** | **Better** ‚úì | **Less** |
| Slow | Large | Poor | More |

**Why fast freezing better?**
- Many nucleation sites ‚Üí Small crystals
- Less cell wall rupture
- Less drip loss on thawing
- Better texture retention

**Slow freezing problems:**
- Few nucleation sites ‚Üí Large crystals
- Pierce cell walls
- More drip loss
- Mushy texture after thawing

**Answer: (B) Small ice crystals**

### Q6. Blanching Purpose (GATE 2022) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
The primary purpose of blanching vegetables before freezing is:

(A) To remove dirt  
(B) To inactivate enzymes  
(C) To add flavor  
(D) To increase moisture

**Solution:**

**Blanching:** Brief heat treatment (80-100¬∞C, 1-5 min)

**Purposes:**
1. **Inactivate enzymes** (peroxidase, polyphenol oxidase) ‚úì
2. Remove air from tissues
3. Soften tissue (better packing)
4. Fix bright color
5. Reduce microbial load

**If not blanched:**
- Enzymes cause off-flavors
- Color deterioration
- Texture breakdown
- Nutritional loss

**Methods:**
- Water blanching (most common)
- Steam blanching
- Microwave blanching

**Answer: (B) To inactivate enzymes**

### Q8. Hurdle Technology (GATE 2021) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Hurdle technology in food preservation involves:

(A) Single preservation method  
(B) Multiple preservation methods  
(C) Only chemical preservatives  
(D) Only physical methods

**Solution:**

**Hurdle technology:**
- **Combination of multiple preservation factors** ("hurdles")
- Each hurdle stresses microorganisms
- Combined effect greater than individual

**Common hurdles:**
1. Temperature (refrigeration, heating)
2. pH (acidification)
3. Water activity (drying, salt, sugar)
4. Preservatives (sorbate, benzoate)
5. Modified atmosphere
6. Competitive flora (fermentation)

**Example:** Jam
- High sugar (low aw)
- Low pH (citric acid)
- Heat treatment
- Sealed container

**Answer: (B) Multiple preservation methods**

### Q9. Relative Humidity in Storage (GATE 2020) ‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question:**  
If storage RH is 75% and product aw is 0.60, the product will:

(A) Gain moisture  
(B) Lose moisture  
(C) Remain in equilibrium  
(D) Spoil immediately

**Solution:**

**Equilibrium concept:**
$$
a_w = \frac{ERH}{100}
$$

where ERH = Equilibrium Relative Humidity

**Given:**
- Storage RH = 75%
- Product aw = 0.60

**Product equilibrium RH:**
$$
ERH = a_w \times 100 = 0.60 \times 100 = 60\%
$$

**Comparison:**
- Storage RH (75%) > Product ERH (60%)
- Environment has more moisture
- **Product will GAIN moisture**

**Answer: (A) Gain moisture**

**Rule:** 
- RH > ERH ‚Üí Product gains moisture
- RH < ERH ‚Üí Product loses moisture
- RH = ERH ‚Üí Equilibrium

### Q2. Acidity in Milk (GATE 2024) ‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Acidity of milk is expressed as percent:

(A) Citric acid  
(B) Acetic acid  
(C) Lactic acid  
(D) Tartaric acid

**Solution:**

**Milk acidity convention:**
- **Expressed as % lactic acid**
- Even though natural acidity is from casein, phosphates, citrates
- When milk sours, lactic acid bacteria produce lactic acid

**Formula:**
$$
\text{Acidity (\%)} = \frac{V \times N \times 0.009}{W} \times 100
$$

where:
- V = Titration volume (ml)
- N = Normality of NaOH
- 0.009 = Milliequivalent weight of lactic acid (g/ml)
- W = Sample weight (g)

**Fresh milk:** 0.14-0.16% lactic acid
**Sour milk:** > 0.18%

**Answer: (C) Lactic acid**

### Q3. HACCP Critical Control Point (GATE 2023) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
In HACCP, CCP stands for:

(A) Critical Chemical Point  
(B) Critical Control Point  
(C) Control Check Point  
(D) Chemical Control Point

**Solution:**

**HACCP:** Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points

**CCP = Critical Control Point**
- Step in process where control can be applied
- Essential to prevent/eliminate food safety hazard
- Or reduce to acceptable level

**Seven HACCP Principles:**
1. Conduct hazard analysis
2. Determine CCPs
3. Establish critical limits
4. Monitoring procedures
5. Corrective actions
6. Verification procedures
7. Record-keeping

**Example CCPs:**
- Pasteurization (temperature/time)
- Metal detection
- Cooling rate
- pH control

**Answer: (B) Critical Control Point**

### Q5. Moisture Determination Method (GATE 2022) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
The standard method for moisture determination in foods is:

(A) Karl Fischer  
(B) Oven drying  
(C) Microwave  
(D) NIR spectroscopy

**Solution:**

**Standard method: Oven drying at 105¬∞C**

**Procedure:**
1. Weigh sample
2. Dry in oven at 103-105¬∞C
3. Cool in desiccator
4. Weigh again
5. Repeat until constant weight

**Calculation:**
$$
\text{Moisture (wb\%)} = \frac{W_{wet} - W_{dry}}{W_{wet}} \times 100
$$

**Other methods:**
- Karl Fischer: Very accurate (for oils)
- Microwave: Fast (not standard)
- NIR: Quick screening (needs calibration)

**Answer: (B) Oven drying**

(Same concept as grain drying - 105¬∞C standard!)

### Q6. Protein by Kjeldahl (GATE 2022) ‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question:**  
In Kjeldahl method, the conversion factor for milk protein is:

(A) 5.7  
(B) 6.25  
(C) 6.38  
(D) 6.50

**Solution:**

**Kjeldahl method:** Measures total nitrogen, converts to protein

**General formula:**
$$
\text{Protein (\%)} = \text{N (\%)} \times \text{Factor}
$$

**Conversion factors:**

| Food | Factor | Reason |
|------|--------|--------|
| General | 6.25 | Average 16% N in protein |
| **Milk** | **6.38** | **Casein-rich** ‚úì |
| Wheat | 5.70 | Gluten proteins |
| Rice | 5.95 | Different amino acid profile |

**Why different?**
- Proteins have different amino acid compositions
- Different % of nitrogen
- Milk proteins: ~15.7% N ‚Üí Factor = 100/15.7 = 6.38

**Answer: (C) 6.38**

### Q7. Adulteration Detection (GATE 2021) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Formalin in milk can be detected by:

(A) Iodine test  
(B) Hansa Mukherjee test  
(C) COB test  
(D) Rosolic acid test

**Solution:**

**Common milk adulteration tests:**

| Adulterant | Test |
|------------|------|
| **Formalin** | **Rosolic acid** ‚úì |
| Urea | Urease test |
| Starch | Iodine test |
| Neutralizers | COB test, Rosolic acid |
| Sugar | Resorcinol test |

**Rosolic acid test (Hehner test):**
- Rosolic acid + HCl added to milk
- **Red ring appears** if formalin present
- Formalin used illegally to preserve milk

**Answer: (D) Rosolic acid test**

### Q8. Sensory Evaluation (GATE 2020) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
In sensory evaluation, the 9-point hedonic scale is used for:

(A) Difference testing  
(B) Preference testing  
(C) Threshold testing  
(D) Ranking testing

**Solution:**

**9-Point Hedonic Scale:**

9. Like extremely
8. Like very much
7. Like moderately
6. Like slightly
5. Neither like nor dislike
4. Dislike slightly
3. Dislike moderately
2. Dislike very much
1. Dislike extremely

**Purpose: Preference/Acceptance testing** ‚úì

**Other methods:**
- **Triangle test:** Difference (identify odd sample)
- **Duo-trio test:** Difference (match to reference)
- **Ranking:** Order by preference
- **Threshold:** Detect minimum concentration

**Answer: (B) Preference testing**

### Q1. Kick's Law (GATE 2024) ‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question:**  
According to Kick's law, energy required for size reduction is proportional to:

(A) Reduction ratio  
(B) Log of reduction ratio  
(C) Square of reduction ratio  
(D) Inverse of particle size

**Solution:**

**Kick's Law:**
$$
E = K_k \times \log\left(\frac{D_i}{D_f}\right)
$$

where Di/Df = Reduction ratio

**Energy proportional to:** **log(Reduction ratio)**

**Answer: (B) Log of reduction ratio**

### Q2. Rittinger's Law Calculation (GATE 2024) ‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Material is crushed from 50 mm to 10 mm. Using Rittinger's law with Kr = 100 kJ/mm, energy required (in kJ) is:

(A) 4  
(B) 8  
(C) 12  
(D) 16

**Solution:**

**Rittinger's Law:**
$$
E = K_r \times \left(\frac{1}{D_f} - \frac{1}{D_i}\right)
$$

Given:
- Di = 50 mm
- Df = 10 mm
- Kr = 100 kJ/mm

$$
E = 100 \times \left(\frac{1}{10} - \frac{1}{50}\right)
$$
$$
= 100 \times (0.1 - 0.02) = 100 \times 0.08 = 8 \text{ kJ}
$$

**Answer: (B) 8 kJ**

Bond's law formula includes:

**Bond's Law:**
$$
E = E_i \times \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{D_f}} - \frac{1}{\sqrt{D_i}}\right)
$$

where Ei = Bond work index

**Contains:** **Square root terms** (‚àöD in denominator)

**Answer: (B) Square root terms**

### Q5. Screen Efficiency (GATE 2023) ‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question:**  
A screen has 80% of undersize in feed and 95% undersize in underflow. Screen efficiency is:

(A) 15%  
(B) 75%  
(C) 85%  
(D) 95%

**Solution:**

**Screen efficiency:**
$$
\eta = \frac{\text{Undersize in underflow}}{\text{Undersize in feed}} \times 100
$$

Actually, correct formula considers recovery:

$$
\eta = \frac{a(b-f)}{f(a-f)} \times 100
$$

But simplified for this problem:
- Feed undersize = 80%
- Underflow undersize = 95%

If 95% of what passes is undersize (from 80% feed):
Efficiency ‚âà 95% (quality of separation)

**Answer: (D) 95%**

### Q6. Reduction Ratio (GATE 2022) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
Feed size = 40 mm, product size = 5 mm. Reduction ratio is:

(A) 4  
(B) 6  
(C) 8  
(D) 10

**Solution:**

**Reduction ratio:**
$$
R = \frac{D_i}{D_f}
$$

Given:
- Di = 40 mm
- Df = 5 mm

$$
R = \frac{40}{5} = 8
$$

**Answer: (C) 8**

### Q7. Energy Comparison (GATE 2022) ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question:**  
For crushing from 10 mm to 1 mm, which law gives maximum energy requirement?

(A) Kick's law  
(B) Rittinger's law  
(C) Bond's law  
(D) All give same

**Solution:**

For **fine grinding** (Di/Df ratio high):

**Relative magnitudes:**
- **Rittinger > Bond > Kick** (for fine grinding)

**For coarse crushing:**
- Kick > Bond > Rittinger

**For 10:1 reduction (fine):** Rittinger highest

**Answer: (B) Rittinger's law**

**Rule:** Rittinger for fine, Kick for coarse

### Q10. Screen Aperture (GATE 2020) ‚≠ê

**Question:**  
A screen mesh number indicates:

(A) Wire diameter  
(B) Number of openings per inch  
(C) Total open area  
(D) Screen thickness

**Solution:**

**Mesh number = Number of openings per linear inch**

Examples:
- 4 mesh = 4 openings/inch = ~6 mm opening
- 20 mesh = 20 openings/inch = ~0.85 mm
- 100 mesh = 100 openings/inch = ~0.15 mm

**Higher mesh number = Finer screen**

**Answer: (B) Number of openings per inch**

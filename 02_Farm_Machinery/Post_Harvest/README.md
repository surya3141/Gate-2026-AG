## üßπ Grain Cleaners

### Purpose

Remove impurities from grain to improve:
- Market value
- Storage quality
- Milling efficiency
- Seed quality

### Types of Impurities

**1. Foreign Materials:**
- Straw, chaff, stems
- Stones, mud balls
- Metal pieces

**2. Damaged Grains:**
- Broken, cracked
- Insect-damaged
- Diseased

**3. Other Seeds:**
- Weed seeds
- Other crop seeds

---

### Winnower

#### Description

Simple machine using air stream to separate light impurities from grain.

#### Working Principle

**Differential settling velocity:**
- Heavy grain: Falls faster
- Light chaff: Blown away by air
- Based on terminal velocity differences

#### Components

**1. Hopper:**
- Receives uncleaned grain
- With adjustable gate

**2. Feed Roller:**
- Controls grain flow
- Ensures uniform feeding

**3. Fan/Blower:**
- Creates air stream
- Hand or motor-driven
- Air velocity: 3-8 m/s

**4. Oscillating Sieves:**
- Coarse sieve on top
- Fine sieve below
- Reciprocating motion

**5. Separation Chambers:**
- Grain outlet (heavy)
- Chaff outlet (light)
- Intermediate outlet

---

#### Operation

**Step 1:** Grain fed from hopper

**Step 2:** Falls through air stream
- Chaff blown to side
- Grain continues down

**Step 3:** Falls on oscillating sieves
- Large impurities retained on coarse sieve
- Grain passes through
- Small impurities pass through fine sieve

**Step 4:** Collection
- Clean grain collected
- Chaff/straw collected separately
- Small impurities collected

---

#### Specifications

|-----------|-------|
| **Capacity** | 500-2000 kg/h |
| **Power** | 1-3 HP (motorized) |
| **Fan speed** | 300-800 rpm |
| **Air velocity** | 3-8 m/s |
| **Cleaning efficiency** | 85-95% |
| **Sieve oscillation** | 180-250 strokes/min |

### Multi-Stage Grain Cleaner

#### Description

Advanced cleaner with multiple cleaning stages for high-quality output.

#### Stages

**Stage 1: Scalping (Pre-cleaning)**
- Removes coarse impurities
- Large straw, stones
- Coarse screen (12-20 mm openings)

**Stage 2: Air Aspiration**
- Removes dust, chaff
- Vertical or horizontal air stream
- Adjustable air velocity

**Stage 3: Screening**
- Multiple sieves
- Different opening sizes
- Separates by size

**Stage 4: Specific Gravity Separation**
- Air classifier or gravity table
- Separates by density
- Removes light/hollow grains

**Stage 5: Final Air Cleaning**
- Removes remaining dust
- Polish cleaning

---

#### Sieve Selection

**For wheat cleaning:**
- Top sieve: 8-10 mm (removes large impurities)
- Middle sieve: 6 mm (retains wheat)
- Bottom sieve: 2-3 mm (removes small seeds)

**Principle:**
- Sieve opening should be 60-75% of grain width
- Allows good grains to pass
- Retains larger/smaller impurities

---

### Gravity Separator

#### Working Principle

Separates materials based on **specific gravity** (density) differences.

#### Design

**Inclined vibrating deck:**
- Perforated surface
- Angle: 2-6¬∞ from horizontal
- Vibration: 200-300 vibrations/min

**Air flow:**
- Upward through perforations
- Fluidizes material
- Lighter particles lifted more

**Result:**
- Heavy particles: Move upward (against slope)
- Light particles: Move downward (with slope)
- Separation into different outlets

---

#### Applications

**1. Seed Cleaning:**
- Remove light/immature seeds
- Remove insect-damaged seeds
- Improve seed quality

**2. Grain Cleaning:**
- Remove stones (heavier)
- Remove hollow/shriveled grains (lighter)
- Remove mud balls

**3. Coffee/Spice Processing:**
- Separate by maturity
- Remove defects

---

#### Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Capacity** | 500-5000 kg/h |
| **Deck inclination** | 2-6¬∞ |
| **Vibration frequency** | 200-300 vpm |
| **Air velocity** | 0.5-2 m/s |
| **Separation efficiency** | 90-98% |
| **Power** | 2-5 HP |

### Destoner

#### Purpose

Remove stones and heavy impurities from grain.

#### Working Principle

**Density and aerodynamic differences:**
- Stones: Heavier, sink in fluidized bed

**Perforated deck:**
- Upward air flow
- Vibrating motion
- Slight inclination

**Operation:**
- Mixture fed onto deck
- Air fluidizes material
- Vibration stratifies layers
- Stones move to collection point
- Grain moves opposite direction

#### Specifications

| Parameter | Value |

### Magnetic Separator

#### Purpose

Remove ferrous (iron/steel) contaminants from grain.

#### Types

**1. Drum Type:**
- Rotating magnetic drum
- Grain flows over
- Metal pieces stick to drum
- Scraped off at back

**2. Plate Type:**

- Metal attracted to plate

## üî• Dryers

### Need for Drying

**Freshly harvested grain moisture:** 20-30%
**Safe storage moisture:** 10-14%

**If not dried:**
- ‚ùå Mold growth
- ‚ùå Insect infestation
- ‚ùå Heating and spoilage
- ‚ùå Weight loss

### Drying Methods

#### 1. Natural/Sun Drying

**Process:**
- Spread grain in thin layer
- Expose to sun and wind
- Turn periodically

**Advantages:**
- No cost
- No energy
- Simple

**Disadvantages:**
- Weather dependent
- Slow (3-7 days)
- Large area needed
- Quality loss (discoloration, contamination)
- Labor intensive
- Loss to birds, rodents

**Drying rate:** 1-2% moisture/day

---

#### 2. Mechanical Drying

**Process:**
- Heated air blown through grain
- Controlled conditions
- Rapid drying

**Advantages:**
- Weather independent
- Fast (hours)
- Better quality
- Less loss

**Disadvantages:**
- High cost
- Energy intensive
- Requires skill

---

### Drying Theory

#### Moisture Content

**Wet basis (wb):**
$$
MC_{wb} = \frac{W_w}{W_w + W_d} \times 100\%
$$

**Dry basis (db):**
$$
MC_{db} = \frac{W_w}{W_d} \times 100\%
$$

Where:
- W_w = Weight of water
- W_d = Weight of dry matter

**Conversion:**
$$
MC_{db} = \frac{MC_{wb}}{100 - MC_{wb}} \times 100
$$

$$
MC_{wb} = \frac{MC_{db}}{100 + MC_{db}} \times 100
$$

---

#### Example Conversion

**Given:** 20% moisture content (wet basis)

**Convert to dry basis:**
$$
MC_{db} = \frac{20}{100-20} \times 100 = \frac{20}{80} \times 100 = 25\%
$$

**Verification:**
In 100 kg grain:
- Water: 20 kg
- Dry matter: 80 kg
- MC (wb) = 20/100 = 20% ‚úì
- MC (db) = 20/80 = 25% ‚úì

---

#### Water to Remove

**Formula:**
$$
W_r = W_i \times \frac{M_i - M_f}{100 - M_f}
$$

Where:
- W_r = Water to remove (kg)
- W_i = Initial weight (kg)
- M_i = Initial MC% (wb)
- M_f = Final MC% (wb)

**Example:**
Remove moisture from 1000 kg grain at 20% MC to 14% MC

$$
W_r = 1000 \times \frac{20-14}{100-14} = 1000 \times \frac{6}{86} = 69.77 \text{ kg}
$$

Final weight = 1000 - 69.77 = 930.23 kg

---

#### Heat Required

**Sensible heat:**
$$
Q_s = m \times C_p \times \Delta T
$$

**Latent heat (evaporation):**
$$
Q_L = W_r \times L
$$

**Total heat:**
$$
Q_{total} = Q_s + Q_L
$$

Where:
- m = Mass (kg)
- C_p = Specific heat (~1.8-2.5 kJ/kg¬∞C for grain)
- ŒîT = Temperature rise (¬∞C)
- W_r = Water removed (kg)
- L = Latent heat of vaporization (~2500 kJ/kg)

---

### Types of Dryers

#### 1. Batch Dryer

**Description:**

#### 2. Continuous Flow Dryer

**Description:**
- Grain continuously fed
- Dried grain continuously discharged
- Uninterrupted operation

**Types:**

**A. Concurrent Flow:**
- Grain and air move same direction
- High temperature possible
- Fast drying

**B. Counter Flow:**
- Grain and air move opposite directions
- More uniform drying
- Moderate temperature

**C. Cross Flow:**
- Air moves perpendicular to grain flow
- Most common
- Column dryer type

---

### Column Dryer (Detailed)

#### Design

**Structure:**
- Tall rectangular column
- Two perforated walls (inner and outer)
- Grain flows down between walls
- Hot air blows horizontally through grain

**Sections:**
1. **Tempering section (top)**
   - Grain enters
   - Initial heating
   
2. **Drying section (middle)**
   - Main drying occurs
   - Hot air passes through
   
3. **Cooling section (bottom)**
   - Ambient air cools grain
   - Prevents moisture reabsorption

**Discharge:**
- Adjustable metering device at bottom
- Controls flow rate = controls drying time

---

#### Working

**Step 1:** Wet grain fed at top

**Step 2:** Grain flows down by gravity

**Step 3:** Hot air (60-90¬∞C) blown through grain
- Moisture removed
- Carried away by exhaust air

**Step 4:** Dried grain passes to cooling section

**Step 5:** Cool air blown through

**Step 6:** Dried, cooled grain discharged

---

#### Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Capacity** | 2-20 tons/h |
| **Drying temperature** | 60-90¬∞C (paddy/wheat) |
| **Cooling air** | Ambient |
| **Moisture reduction** | 4-8% in single pass |
| **Grain temperature** | Max 50¬∞C (seed), 60¬∞C (commercial) |
| **Power** | 10-50 HP |
| **Fuel** | Rice husk, diesel, LPG |

---

### Fluidized Bed Dryer

#### Principle

Grain suspended in upward air stream (fluidized state) for rapid drying.

#### Design

**Perforated bed:**
- Grain layer (10-30 cm)
- High-velocity air from below
- Grain particles suspended

**Characteristics:**
- Very fast drying (30-60 min)
- Uniform drying
- Gentle handling
- High energy consumption

---

#### Applications

- Seed drying (uniform, gentle)
- High-moisture grain (>25%)
- Tempering (pre-drying stage)

**Drying rate:** Up to 3-5% moisture/hour

---

### Recirculating Batch Dryer

#### Concept

Combines drying and tempering in cycles.

#### Process

**Cycle 1:**
- Dry for 30-60 minutes
- Grain heated, moisture moves to surface

**Tempering (rest period):**
- Air off, grain stays in dryer
- Moisture equalizes within kernel
- Prevents stress cracks

**Cycle 2:**
- Dry again
- Repeat until target moisture

**Advantages:**
- Better quality
- Less cracking
- Uniform moisture

---

---

### Dryer Performance

#### Drying Efficiency

$$
\eta = \frac{\text{Heat used for evaporation}}{\text{Total heat supplied}} \times 100\%
$$

**Typical:** 40-60%

**Losses:**
- Exhaust air heat
- Radiation
- Heating dryer structure

---

#### Drying Rate

$$
\text{Drying rate} = \frac{\text{Moisture removed (\%)}}{\text{Time (h)}}
$$

**Example:** 20% to 14% MC in 4 hours
$$
\text{Rate} = \frac{20-14}{4} = 1.5\% \text{ per hour}
$$

---

#### Specific Energy Consumption

$$
\text{SEC (kJ/kg water)} = \frac{\text{Total energy input (kJ)}}{\text{Water removed (kg)}}
$$

**Typical values:**
- Efficient dryers: 4000-6000 kJ/kg water
- Poor dryers: 8000-12000 kJ/kg water

**Theoretical minimum:** ~2500 kJ/kg (latent heat only)

- O‚ÇÇ depletes naturally

## üçö Parboiling Equipment

### What is Parboiling?

**Process:** Hydrothermal treatment of paddy before milling

**Steps:**
1. Soaking (in hot water)
2. Steaming (under pressure)
3. Drying

**Purpose:**
- Increase head rice yield
- Improve nutritional value (vitamins diffuse into endosperm)
- Harden grain (less breakage)
- Easier milling

---

### Parboiling Methods

#### 1. Traditional Method

**Steps:**
1. Soak paddy in cold water (2-3 days)
2. Drain and steam in large vessel
3. Sun dry

**Disadvantages:**
- Long time
- Unhygienic
- Inconsistent quality
- High labor

---

#### 2. Modern Parboiling

**Hot Soak Method:**

**Equipment:**
- Soaking tank (with heating)
- Pressure steamer
- Mechanical dryer

**Process:**

**Step 1: Soaking**
- Duration: 3-6 hours (hot water 60-70¬∞C)
- Or: 20-24 hours (cold water)
- Moisture increases: 12% ‚Üí 30%

**Step 2: Steaming**
- Pressure: 1-2 bar
- Temperature: 100-120¬∞C
- Duration: 10-20 minutes
- Starch gelatinizes

**Step 3: Drying**
- Initial: Sun or mechanical
- To 18-20% MC
- Then: Final drying to 14%
- Slow cooling

---

### Parboiling Equipment

#### 1. Soaking Tank

**Design:**
- Capacity: 2-10 tons
- Steam coils or jackets for heating
- Temperature control
- Overflow drain

**Operation:**
- Fill with water
- Heat to 60-70¬∞C
- Add paddy
- Soak 3-6 hours
- Drain

---

#### 2. Steaming Vessel

**Types:**

**A. Vertical Steamer:**
- Cylindrical vessel
- Paddy in perforated baskets
- Steam from bottom
- Batch operation

**B. Horizontal Steamer:**
- Rotating drum
- Continuous operation
- Even steaming

**Specifications:**
- Pressure: 1-2 bar (15-30 psi)
- Temperature: 105-120¬∞C
- Time: 15-20 minutes

---

#### 3. Parboiling Dryer

**Similar to paddy dryer but:**
- Lower temperature (45-50¬∞C)
- Longer time
- Prevent case hardening
- Multiple stages

### Rice Milling

#### Process Flow

```
PADDY ‚Üí CLEANING ‚Üí DESTONING ‚Üí DEHUSKING ‚Üí 
PADDY SEPARATION ‚Üí WHITENING ‚Üí POLISHING ‚Üí GRADING
```

---

### 1. Paddy Cleaner

(Same as grain cleaner - described earlier)
- Removes straw, dust, mud
- Pre-cleaning essential

---

### 2. Destoner

(Described in cleaning section)
- Removes stones
- Protects sheller

---

### 3. Paddy Sheller/Dehusker

#### Purpose

Remove husk (outer covering) from paddy to get brown rice.

#### Types

**A. Rubber Roll Sheller:**

**Design:**
- Two parallel rubber rolls
- Rotate at different speeds
- Gap adjustable

**Working:**
- Paddy fed between rolls
- Differential speed creates shearing
- Husk cracked and removed
- Brown rice intact

**Specifications:**
- Roll diameter: 20-30 cm
- Speed difference: 2.5:1 (fast:slow ratio)
- Gap: 1-2 mm
- Capacity: 500-2000 kg/h
- Dehusking: 85-95%

---

**B. Disc Sheller:**

**Design:**
- Two abrasive discs
- One stationary, one rotating
- Paddy fed at center

**Working:**
- Centrifugal force throws paddy outward
- Friction between discs removes husk
- Mixed output at periphery

**Specifications:**
- Disc diameter: 30-50 cm
- Speed: 1200-1500 rpm
- Capacity: 800-1500 kg/h
- Dehusking: 75-85%

---

### 4. Paddy Separator

#### Purpose

Separate unhusked paddy from brown rice for re-hulling.

#### Principle

**Density difference:**
- Paddy (with husk): Lighter (1100 kg/m¬≥)
- Brown rice (husk removed): Heavier (1400 kg/m¬≥)

#### Design - Paddy Separator Table

**Inclined vibrating surface:**
- Paddy (lighter) flows upward
- Rice (heavier) flows downward
- Upward air assist

**Efficiency:** 95-98%

---

### 5. Rice Whitener

#### Purpose

Remove bran layer from brown rice to produce white rice.

#### Types

**A. Abrasive Whitener:**

**Design:**
- Rotating abrasive roll/disc
- Screen surrounding
- Brown rice fed tangentially

**Working:**
- Abrasive surface rubs rice
- Bran layer peeled off
- Passes through screen
- White rice exits

**Whitening degree:** Controlled by time/pressure

---

**B. Friction Whitener:**

**Design:**
- Horizontal or vertical chamber
- Rotating impeller
- Perforated screen

**Working:**
- Rice-to-rice friction
- Bran removed by rubbing
- Less grain breakage than abrasive

**Specifications:**
- Chamber diameter: 20-40 cm
- Speed: 900-1200 rpm
- Capacity: 500-1500 kg/h
- Whitening: 8-12% weight removal

---

### 6. Rice Polisher

#### Purpose

Give glossy appearance to white rice.

#### Working

**Water or oil sprayed on rice:**
- Moisture (1-2%)
- Rotating brush rubs
- Surface smoothed
- Shiny appearance

**Not essential but improves market appeal**

---

### 7. Rice Grader

**Separates by size:**
- Head rice (full grain)
- Large broken (>3/4 grain)
- Medium broken (1/2 to 3/4)
- Small broken (<1/2)

**Screen grader:** Multiple sieves with different openings

---

### Rice Milling Yield

**Milling recovery:**
$$
\text{Milling recovery (\%)} = \frac{\text{Weight of rice}}{\text{Weight of paddy}} \times 100
$$

**Typical values:**

| Product | Yield from Paddy |
|---------|------------------|
| **Brown rice** | 80-82% |
| **Milled white rice** | 68-72% |
| **Head rice (whole)** | 55-65% (depends on variety, processing) |
| **Broken rice** | 10-15% |
| **Husk** | 18-20% |
| **Bran** | 8-10% |

**Head Rice Recovery (HRR):**
$$
\text{HRR (\%)} = \frac{\text{Weight of head rice}}{\text{Weight of paddy}} \times 100
$$

**Higher HRR = Better quality + Higher price**

### Wheat Milling

#### Process

```
WHEAT ‚Üí CLEANING ‚Üí TEMPERING ‚Üí GRINDING ‚Üí 
SIEVING ‚Üí PURIFICATION ‚Üí FLOUR
```

---

#### Tempering

**Purpose:** Add moisture to wheat (14-16%) for 8-24 hours

**Benefits:**
- Softens bran (easier to separate)
- Toughens endosperm (less pulverizing)
- Better flour quality

---

#### Roller Mill

**Design:**
- Pair of counter-rotating rollers
- Fluted (breaking) or smooth (reduction)
- Adjustable gap

**Working:**
- Wheat passes through
- Crushed/ground progressively
- Multiple stages (4-6)

**Products:** Coarse flour, fine flour, semolina, bran

---

#### Plansifter

**Sieving machine:**
- Multiple sieves stacked
- Gyratory motion
- Separates flour by particle size

---

#### Purifier

**Separates bran from flour:**
- Oscillating sieves
- Air current
- Light bran blown away
- Heavy flour retained

---

| **Bran** | 18-22% |
| **Husk/loss** | 4-8% |

**Extraction rate:** Percentage of wheat converted to flour

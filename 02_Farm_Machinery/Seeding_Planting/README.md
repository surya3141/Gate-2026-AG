## 1. Introduction to Seeding

### 1.1 Objectives of Good Seeding

**Primary Objectives:**
- Place seeds at proper depth
- Maintain uniform spacing
- Ensure good seed-soil contact
- Optimize plant population
- Minimize seed damage
- Achieve desired emergence

---

### 1.2 Methods of Seeding

| Method | Description | Equipment | Crops |
|--------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| **Broadcasting** | Random scattering | Broadcaster | Wheat, Rice |
| **Drilling** | Continuous rows | Seed drill | Cereals, Pulses |
| **Dibbling** | Holes at spacing | Dibbler | Maize, Cotton |
| **Precision Planting** | Individual seeds | Planter | Maize, Soybean |
| **Transplanting** | Seedlings | Transplanter | Rice, Tobacco |

---

### 1.3 Seed Placement Requirements

**Depth of Sowing:**
- Small seeds (1-2 cm): Mustard, Millet
- Medium seeds (3-5 cm): Wheat, Barley
- Large seeds (5-8 cm): Maize, Soybean

**Spacing:**
- **Row spacing:** Distance between rows
- **Plant spacing:** Distance between plants in row
- **Seed rate:** kg/ha or seeds/ha

---

## 2. Seed Drills

### 2.1 Definition and Components

**Seed Drill:** Machine for sowing seeds in continuous rows at uniform depth.

**Main Components:**

1. **Frame** - Main structure
2. **Seed hopper** - Stores seeds
3. **Seed metering device** - Controls seed rate
4. **Furrow openers** - Makes furrows
5. **Seed tubes** - Conveys seeds
6. **Covering device** - Covers seeds
7. **Ground wheel** - Drives mechanism
8. **Markers** - Marks next row

---

### 2.2 Types of Seed Drills

**a) Plain Seed Drill:**
- Only for seeding
- No fertilizer attachment
- Simple construction

**b) Fertilizer-cum-Seed Drill:**
- Separate hoppers for seed and fertilizer
- Places fertilizer below seeds
- Most common type

**c) Multi-crop Seed Drill:**
- Handles various seed sizes
- Adjustable metering
- Versatile use

**d) Zero-till Seed Drill:**
- For conservation agriculture
- Direct seeding without tillage
- Heavy-duty furrow openers

---

### 2.3 Furrow Opener Types

| Type | Shape | Use | Soil Type |
|------|-------|-----|-----------|
| **Shoe type** | Flat blade | General | Light to medium |
| **Hoe type** | Angled blade | Heavy soil | Clay, hard |
| **Inverted-T** | T-shaped | Precision | Medium |
| **Disc type** | Concave disc | Trashy | All types |
| **Triple disc** | 3 discs | Minimal disturbance | Conservation tillage |

**Disc Furrow Opener:**
- Diameter: 35-45 cm
- Angle: 8-12¬∞
- Better in trashy conditions
- Less draft

---

### 2.4 Seed Drill Specifications

**Standard Specifications:**
- Number of rows: 7-13
- Row spacing: 15-23 cm (adjustable)
- Seed rate: 5-100 kg/ha
- Working depth: 2-10 cm
- Working width: 2-3 m
- Draft: 400-800 N
- Speed: 3-5 km/h
- Tractor power: 25-35 HP

## 3. Seed Metering Mechanisms

### 3.1 Classification

**Based on Principle:**

**a) Force Feed Type:**
- **Fluted roller** - Most common in seed drills
- **Internal fluted roller** - For small seeds
- **Cell type** - Precision metering

**b) Gravity Feed Type:**
- **Studded roller** - For large seeds
- **Brush type** - For small, round seeds

---

### 3.2 Fluted Roller Mechanism

**Working Principle:**
- Rotating fluted roller picks seeds
- Seeds fall through opening
- Rotation controls seed rate

**Design Parameters:**

**Effective length of flute:**
$$
L = N \times p
$$
Where:
- L = Effective length (cm)
- N = Number of flutes exposed
- p = Pitch of flute (cm)

**Seed rate:**
$$
Q = \frac{60 \times A \times L \times N_r \times \rho}{10^6}
$$

Where:
- Q = Seed rate (kg/ha)
- A = Cross-sectional area of flute (cm¬≤)
- L = Effective length (cm)
- N_r = Roller RPM
- œÅ = Bulk density of seed (kg/m¬≥)

**Simplified Formula:**
$$
\text{Seed rate} = K \times L \times N
$$

Where:
- K = Constant for seed type
- L = Effective length
- N = Number of rows

---

### 3.3 Cell Type Metering

**Used in:** Precision planters

**Types:**
- Horizontal plate
- Vertical plate
- Edge cell (inclined plate)

**Working:**
- Individual cells pick single seeds
- Vacuum or mechanical singulation
- Precise spacing

**Cell Size:**
$$
\text{Cell diameter} = (1.2 \text{ to } 1.5) \times \text{Seed diameter}
$$

---

### 3.4 Vacuum Metering

**Principle:**
- Vacuum holds seed to cell
- Seed released at planting position
- Very precise

**Vacuum Pressure:**
- Typical: 40-80 kPa
- Too low: Seed drops
- Too high: Multiple seeds

**Advantages:**
- High precision
- Suitable for irregular seeds
- Minimal seed damage

## 4. Planters

### 4.1 Definition and Difference from Drill

**Planter:** Machine for precision planting of seeds at fixed spacing.

**Drill vs Planter:**

| Feature | Seed Drill | Planter |
|---------|------------|---------|
| **Seed placement** | Continuous | Hill drop/Single seed |
| **Spacing** | Random in row | Precise |
| **Seeds per hole** | Multiple | 1-3 (precise) |
| **Crops** | Wheat, pulses | Maize, cotton |
| **Metering** | Force feed | Cell type/Vacuum |

---

### 4.2 Types of Planters

**a) Maize Planter:**
- Row spacing: 60-75 cm
- Plant spacing: 15-25 cm
- Seed rate: 18-25 kg/ha
- Precision metering

**b) Cotton Planter:**
- Row spacing: 60-90 cm
- Plant spacing: 10-30 cm
- Fuzzy or delinted seeds

**c) Pneumatic Planter:**
- Vacuum metering
- Very high precision
- For small seeds

**d) No-till Planter:**
- Heavy-duty openers
- Direct planting in residue
- Minimal soil disturbance

---

### 4.3 Planter Components

**Additional Features (vs Seed Drill):**

1. **Precision metering** - Cell type/vacuum
2. **Seed tube** - Individual per row
3. **Depth control** - Gauge wheel per row
4. **Press wheel** - Firms soil over seed
5. **Fertilizer attachment** - Side or below seed
6. **Monitor** - Detects skips/doubles

---

### 4.4 Planting Pattern

**Hill Drop Planting:**
- 2-3 seeds per hill
- Hills at fixed spacing
- Better weed control

**Single Seed Planting:**
- One seed per cell
- Maximum precision
- Requires quality seeds

**Spacing Formula:**
$$
\text{Plant spacing (cm)} = \frac{N_c \times V \times 100}{N_p \times W}
$$

Where:
- N_c = Number of cells on plate
- V = Forward speed (m/s)
- N_p = Plate RPM
- W = Circumference factor

## 5. Transplanting Machines

### 5.1 Rice Transplanter

**Types:**

**a) Manual Transplanter:**
- Manually fed seedlings
- 2-4 rows
- Low cost
- Capacity: 0.1-0.15 ha/h

**b) Riding Type Transplanter:**
- Operator rides on machine
- 4-8 rows
- Self-propelled or tractor-drawn
- Capacity: 0.3-0.5 ha/h

**c) Walking Type Transplanter:**
- Operator walks behind
- 4-6 rows
- Engine-powered
- Capacity: 0.2-0.4 ha/h

---

### 5.2 Transplanter Components

1. **Seedling tray** - Holds mat-type seedlings
2. **Planting mechanism** - Picks and plants
3. **Float** - Maintains depth in puddled field
4. **Planting arms** - Reciprocating or rotary

---

### 5.3 Transplanting Mechanisms

**a) Fork Type:**
- Fork picks seedling
- Plants by pushing into soil
- Simple design

**b) Rotary Type:**
- Rotating mechanism
- Continuous planting
- Higher efficiency

**c) Mat-type Seedling:**
- Pre-grown in trays
- Roots intertwined
- Machine picks from mat

---

### 5.4 Transplanter Specifications

**Standard Specifications:**
- Number of rows: 4-8
- Row spacing: 20-30 cm
- Plant spacing: 12-20 cm
- Planting depth: 2-4 cm
- Seedlings per hill: 2-6
- Working width: 1-2 m
- Field capacity: 0.2-0.5 ha/h
- Seedling age: 15-25 days

**Seedling Requirements:**
- **Mat type:** Grown in tray (30√ó60 cm)
- **Height:** 12-20 cm
- **Root length:** 2-3 cm
- **Seedling density:** 3000-4000 plants/m¬≤

## 7. Dibbling and Broadcasting

### 7.1 Dibbler

**Function:** Makes holes at fixed spacing for seed placement.

**Types:**
- **Manual dibbler:** Hand-operated
- **Animal-drawn dibbler:** 2-4 rows
- **Tractor-mounted dibbler:** Multiple rows

**Specifications:**
- Hole depth: 5-10 cm
- Spacing: Adjustable (20-60 cm)
- Row spacing: 45-90 cm

---

### 7.2 Broadcaster

**Function:** Scatters seeds uniformly over field.

**Types:**

**a) Manual Broadcaster:**
- Hand-cranked
- Coverage: 6-10 m width
- Capacity: 0.4-0.6 ha/h

**b) Centrifugal Broadcaster:**
- Spinning disc
- Tractor PTO-driven
- Coverage: 10-20 m width
- Capacity: 3-5 ha/h

**c) Pneumatic Broadcaster:**
- Air stream distributes seeds
- Very uniform
- 15-25 m width

---

### 7.3 Broadcasting Pattern

**Distribution uniformity:**
$$
\text{CV} = \frac{\sigma}{\mu} \times 100\%
$$

Where:
- CV = Coefficient of variation
- œÉ = Standard deviation
- Œº = Mean seed rate

**Good uniformity:** CV < 15%

**Overlap for uniform coverage:**
$$
\text{Effective width} = 0.7 \times \text{Throw width}
$$

## 8. Performance Parameters

### 8.1 Seed Rate

**Theoretical seed rate:**
$$
Q = \frac{N \times n \times W}{S \times 10}
$$

Where:
- Q = Seed rate (kg/ha)
- N = Seeds per meter row
- n = Number of rows
- W = Weight of 1000 seeds (g)
- S = Row spacing (cm)

**Actual seed rate:**
Measured by collecting seeds over known area.

**Seed rate variation:**
$$
\text{Variation} = \frac{\text{Max - Min}}{\text{Mean}} \times 100\%
$$

Should be < 10% for good metering.

---

### 8.2 Seed Spacing

**Mean spacing:**
$$
\bar{S} = \frac{\sum S_i}{n}
$$

**Spacing uniformity (Quality of Feed Index):**

**Miss Index (M):**
% of spacings > 1.5 √ó mean spacing

**Multiple Index (D):**
% of spacings < 0.5 √ó mean spacing

**Quality Index (Q):**
$$
Q = 100 - (M + D)
$$

**Good planting:** Q > 85%

---

### 8.3 Depth of Planting

**Mean depth:**
$$
\bar{d} = \frac{\sum d_i}{n}
$$

**Coefficient of variation:**
$$
\text{CV} = \frac{\sigma}{\bar{d}} \times 100\%
$$

**Acceptable:** CV < 20%

---

### 8.4 Field Capacity

**Theoretical field capacity:**
$$
\text{TFC} = \frac{W \times S}{10}
$$

**Effective field capacity:**
$$
\text{EFC} = \frac{\text{Area covered}}{\text{Total time}}
$$

**Field efficiency:**
$$
\eta_f = \frac{\text{EFC}}{\text{TFC}} \times 100\%
$$

**Typical values:**
- Seed drill: 70-80%
- Planter: 65-75%
- Transplanter: 60-70%

---

### 8.5 Draft and Power

**Draft of seed drill:**
$$
D = D_f + D_s + D_m
$$

Where:
- D_f = Draft of furrow openers
- D_s = Soil manipulation draft
- D_m = Machine rolling resistance

**Specific draft of furrow opener:**
$$
d = k \times A
$$

Where:
- d = Draft per opener (N)
- k = Soil constant (N/cm¬≤)
- A = Cross-sectional area (cm¬≤)

**Total draft:**
$$
D_{\text{total}} = n \times d
$$
(n = number of furrow openers)

**Power requirement:**
$$
P = \frac{D \times V}{3.6}
$$

---

### 8.6 Emergence and Yield

**Emergence percentage:**
$$
E = \frac{\text{Plants emerged}}{\text{Seeds sown}} \times 100\%
$$

**Plant population:**
$$
P = \frac{10000}{R \times S}
$$

Where:
- P = Plants per hectare
- R = Row spacing (m)
- S = Plant spacing (m)

**Example:** 
R = 0.6 m, S = 0.2 m
$$
P = \frac{10000}{0.6 \times 0.2} = 83,333 \text{ plants/ha}
$$

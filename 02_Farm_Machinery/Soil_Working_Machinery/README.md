## 1. Introduction to Tillage

### 1.1 Definition and Objectives

**Tillage** is the mechanical manipulation of soil for optimizing crop production.

**Main Objectives:**
- Prepare a suitable seedbed
- Control weeds
- Incorporate crop residues
- Improve soil aeration and water infiltration
- Break hardpans and compacted layers
- Modify soil temperature

---

### 1.2 Types of Tillage

| Type | Depth | Purpose | Equipment |
|------|-------|---------|-----------|
| **Primary Tillage** | 15-45 cm | Breaking hard soil, turning furrows | Ploughs, Subsoilers |
| **Secondary Tillage** | 5-15 cm | Seedbed preparation, leveling | Harrows, Cultivators |
| **Deep Tillage** | > 45 cm | Breaking hardpan | Subsoilers, Chisels |

---

### 1.3 Tillage Systems

**Conventional Tillage:** Multiple passes with plough and harrow

**Conservation Tillage:**
- **Minimum Tillage:** Reducing number of operations
- **Zero Tillage:** Direct seeding without tillage
- **Ridge Tillage:** Planting on ridges
- **Mulch Tillage:** Retaining crop residues

---

## 2. Primary Tillage Equipment

### 2.1 Classification

**Based on Power Source:**
- Animal-drawn implements

- Power-driven implements

**Based on Operation:**
- Soil inverting (Mould board plough)
- Soil mixing (Disc plough)
- Soil breaking (Chisel plough)
- Soil cutting (Subsoiler)

---

### 2.2 Design Considerations

**Forces Acting on Tillage Tools:**

$$
D = \frac{1}{2} \rho V^2 C_d A
$$

Where:
- D = Draft force (N)
- œÅ = Soil density (kg/m¬≥)
- V = Forward velocity (m/s)
- C_d = Drag coefficient
- A = Cross-sectional area (m¬≤)

**Draft Equation (Universal Soil Loss Equation):**

$$
D = f[A d(ad + b) + c V^2 A d]
$$

Where:
- f = Soil texture factor
- A = Cross-sectional area of furrow
- d = Depth of operation
- a, b, c = Soil-tool interaction constants
- V = Speed of operation

## 3. Ploughs

### 3.1 Mould Board Plough

**Function:** Cuts, lifts, inverts, and pulverizes soil while burying weeds and crop residues.

**Components:**
1. **Share** - Cuts the furrow slice horizontally
2. **Mould board** - Inverts and pulverizes the soil
3. **Landside** - Provides side thrust support
4. **Frog** - Holds all components together
5. **Coulter** - Makes vertical cut in soil

**Types of Mould Boards:**

| Type | Shape | Scouring | Pulverization | Suitable Soil |
|------|-------|----------|---------------|---------------|
| **Stubble** | Short, steep curve | Excellent | Poor | Sandy, well-drained |
| **General Purpose** | Medium curve | Good | Moderate | Loamy soils |
| **Sod/Breaker** | Long, gentle curve | Fair | Excellent | Heavy clay, sod |
| **Slat** | Spaced bars | Poor | Good | Sticky soils |

**Specifications:**
- Working width: 20-30 cm per bottom
- Depth: 15-30 cm
- Speed: 4-6 km/h
- Draft per bottom: 600-1000 N

---

### 3.2 Disc Plough

**Function:** Cuts, breaks, and partially inverts soil using rotating concave discs.

**Advantages over Mould Board:**
- Works in hard, dry, trashy conditions
- Less draft in hard soils
- Fewer clogging problems
- Self-sharpening action

**Components:**
- Disc blade (concave shape)
- Scraper
- Bearing assembly
- Frame

**Disc Angles:**
- **Disc angle:** 42¬∞ to 45¬∞ (horizontal plane)
- **Tilt angle:** 15¬∞ to 25¬∞ (vertical plane)

**Specifications:**
- Disc diameter: 60-90 cm
- Working depth: 10-25 cm
- Spacing: 25-30 cm
- Speed: 5-8 km/h

**Formulae:**

**Draft of disc plough:**
$$
D = K \times d \times w \times N
$$

Where:
- K = Soil constant (N/cm¬≤)
- d = Depth (cm)
- w = Width of cut per disc (cm)
- N = Number of discs

---

### 3.3 Chisel Plough

**Function:** Deep tillage without inversion, breaking hardpan.

**Features:**
- Narrow curved shanks with pointed shovels
- Depth: 20-60 cm
- Low draft compared to mould board
- Good for conservation tillage

**Applications:**
- Breaking hardpan
- Deep loosening
- Minimum tillage systems

---

### 3.4 Reversible Plough

**Function:** Can throw furrow slice to either side.

**Advantages:**
- No dead furrows or back furrows
- Efficient land use
- Suitable for contour ploughing

**Types:**
- Two-way mould board plough
- Turn-over plough

---

## 4. Secondary Tillage Equipment

### 4.1 Purpose

- Break clods produced by primary tillage
- Level the field
- Prepare fine seedbed
- Control weeds
- Mix soil and fertilizer

---

### 4.2 Classification

| Equipment | Action | Depth | Use |
|-----------|--------|-------|-----|
| **Disc Harrow** | Cutting, mixing | 10-15 cm | Breaking clods |
| **Spike Tooth Harrow** | Scratching, leveling | 5-10 cm | Weed control |
| **Spring Tooth Harrow** | Stirring, aerating | 7-12 cm | Seedbed preparation |
| **Cultivator** | Stirring, weeding | 5-10 cm | Inter-cultivation |
| **Rotavator** | Cutting, mixing | 10-20 cm | Fine tilth |

## 5. Harrows

### 5.1 Disc Harrow

**Types:**

**a) Offset Disc Harrow:**
- Two gangs of discs in tandem
- Gangs offset at angle
- Depth: 10-15 cm

**b) Tandem Disc Harrow:**
- Four gangs in tandem
- Two front, two rear
- Better leveling

**Disc Specifications:**
- Diameter: 40-70 cm
- Disc angle: 15¬∞ to 25¬∞
- Spacing: 20-25 cm
- Concavity: 4-10 cm

**Adjustment Parameters:**
- **Gang angle:** Controls cutting action (15-25¬∞)
- **Tilt angle:** Controls penetration (0-10¬∞)
- **Weight transfer:** Controls depth

---

### 5.2 Spike Tooth Harrow

**Construction:**
- Rigid frame with spike teeth
- Teeth length: 8-12 cm
- Spacing: 10-15 cm

**Types:**
- Fixed tooth
- Flexible tooth (spring loaded)

**Uses:**
- Light seedbed preparation
- Weed control in standing crops
- Spreading manure

---

### 5.3 Spring Tooth Harrow

**Features:**
- Curved spring steel teeth
- Self-cleaning action
- Teeth spacing: 10-15 cm
- Working depth: 7-12 cm

**Advantages:**
- Works in trashy conditions
- Good soil penetration
- Less clogging

---

## 6. Cultivators

### 6.1 Definition and Types

**Cultivator:** Equipment for secondary tillage and inter-cultivation.

**Types:**

**a) Rigid Tine Cultivator:**
- Fixed, rigid shanks
- Shovels at bottom
- Depth: 5-15 cm
- Working width: 1.5-3 m

**b) Spring Tine Cultivator:**
- Flexible curved tines
- Better trash handling
- Self-cleaning

**c) Duck Foot Cultivator:**
- Wide, flat sweeps
- Cuts weeds below surface
- Minimum soil disturbance

---

### 6.2 Components

1. **Frame** - Main structure
2. **Tines/Shanks** - Soil engaging parts
3. **Shovels/Sweeps** - Cutting tools
4. **Depth adjustment** - Wheel or skid
5. **Hitch** - Tractor attachment

---

### 6.3 Shovel Types

| Shovel Type | Width | Use |
|-------------|-------|-----|
| **Narrow Point** | 5-8 cm | Deep penetration, hard soil |
| **Broad Share** | 20-30 cm | Shallow cultivation |
| **Sweep** | 30-60 cm | Weed control |
| **Reversible Point** | 8-15 cm | General purpose |

## 7. Rotavators

### 7.1 Definition and Principle

**Rotavator (Rotary Tiller):** Power-driven tillage implement with rotating blades.

**Working Principle:**
- Blades rotate in direction opposite to travel
- Cuts, pulverizes, and mixes soil in single pass
- PTO-driven from tractor

---

### 7.2 Types

**a) Horizontal Axis Rotavator:**
- Rotor shaft horizontal
- Most common type
- Better mixing

**b) Vertical Axis Rotavator:**
- Rotor shaft vertical
- Less power requirement
- Poor mixing

---

### 7.3 Components

1. **Rotor assembly** - Rotating shaft with flanges
2. **Blades (L or C type)** - Cutting tools
3. **Gear box** - Power transmission
4. **Side covers** - Protect from debris
5. **Rear cover** - Controls soil throw
6. **Skid/Roller** - Depth control

---

### 7.4 Blade Types

**L-Type Blades:**
- Simple L-shaped design
- Lower cost
- General purpose use

**C-Type Blades:**
- Curved, chisel-shaped
- Better penetration
- Longer life

**Blade Arrangement:**
- Offset pattern on flanges
- Spacing: 10-15 cm
- Number: 24-48 blades per rotor

---

### 7.5 Performance Parameters

**Forward Speed (V):**
$$
V = \frac{N \times P \times 60}{1000 \times n}
$$

Where:
- N = PTO speed (rpm)
- P = Blade spacing (cm)
- n = Number of blade impacts per unit distance

**Slip:**
$$
\text{Slip (\%)} = \frac{\text{Theoretical speed - Actual speed}}{\text{Theoretical speed}} \times 100
$$

**Power Requirement:**
$$
P = \frac{D \times V}{3600 \times \eta}
$$

Where:
- P = Power (kW)
- D = Draft (N)
- V = Forward speed (m/s)
- Œ∑ = Transmission efficiency

**Rotor Speed Ratio:**
$$
\lambda = \frac{\text{Peripheral velocity of rotor}}{\text{Forward velocity}} = \frac{\pi D N}{60 V}
$$

Where:
- D = Rotor diameter (m)
- N = Rotor speed (rpm)
- V = Forward speed (m/s)

Typical range: Œª = 10-20

---

### 7.6 Advantages and Limitations

**Advantages:**
- One-pass seedbed preparation
- Excellent pulverization
- Uniform mixing of residues
- Saves time and energy

**Limitations:**
- High power requirement
- Creates hardpan at operating depth
- Destroys soil structure
- Not suitable for very wet/dry soils

**Specifications:**
- Working width: 100-200 cm
- Depth: 10-20 cm
- PTO speed: 540 rpm
- Tractor power: 35-60 HP
- Forward speed: 2-4 km/h

## 8. Subsoilers and Chisels

### 8.1 Subsoiler

**Function:** Break hardpan and compact layers without inversion.

**Features:**
- Heavy, rigid frame
- Narrow, curved shanks
- Operating depth: 45-90 cm
- High draft requirement

**Components:**
- Shank (strong alloy steel)
- Chisel point
- Wing (optional for lateral fracture)

**Use Cases:**
- Breaking compacted layers
- Improving water infiltration
- Deep root penetration

---

### 8.2 Chisel vs Subsoiler

| Parameter | Chisel Plough | Subsoiler |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Depth** | 20-40 cm | 45-90 cm |
| **Spacing** | 20-40 cm | 60-150 cm |
| **Inversion** | Minimal | None |
| **Draft** | Medium | Very high |
| **Use** | Primary tillage | Hardpan breaking |

## 9. Performance Parameters

### 9.1 Field Capacity

**Theoretical Field Capacity (TFC):**
$$
\text{TFC} = \frac{W \times S}{10}
$$

Where:
- TFC = ha/h
- W = Working width (m)
- S = Speed (km/h)

**Effective Field Capacity (EFC):**
$$
\text{EFC} = \text{TFC} \times \text{Field Efficiency}
$$

**Field Efficiency:**
$$
\eta_f = \frac{\text{Effective Field Capacity}}{\text{Theoretical Field Capacity}} \times 100
$$

Typical values:
- Ploughs: 70-85%
- Harrows: 75-90%
- Cultivators: 70-85%
- Rotavators: 75-85%

---

### 9.2 Draft and Power

**Draft:**
- Force required to pull implement
- Measured in N or kN

**Specific Draft:**
$$
\text{Specific Draft} = \frac{\text{Total Draft (N)}}{\text{Cross-sectional area (cm}^2\text{)}}
$$

Unit: N/cm¬≤

**Drawbar Power:**
$$
P_{db} = \frac{D \times V}{3.6}
$$

Where:
- P_db = Power (kW)
- D = Draft (kN)
- V = Speed (km/h)

**PTO Power:**
$$
P_{PTO} = \frac{2 \pi N T}{60000}
$$

Where:
- N = PTO speed (rpm)
- T = Torque (Nm)

---

### 9.3 Energy Requirement

**Specific Energy:**
$$
E_s = \frac{\text{Total Energy (kJ)}}{\text{Volume of soil tilled (m}^3\text{)}}
$$

Unit: kJ/m¬≥

Typical values:
- Mould board plough: 8-15 kJ/m¬≥
- Disc plough: 6-10 kJ/m¬≥
- Rotavator: 12-20 kJ/m¬≥

---

### 9.4 Cost of Operation

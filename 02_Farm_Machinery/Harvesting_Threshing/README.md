## 1. Introduction to Harvesting

### 1.1 Definition and Importance

**Harvesting:** Operation of gathering mature crop from the field.

**Objectives:**
- Harvest at proper maturity
- Minimize losses
- Maintain grain quality
- Maximize efficiency
- Reduce labor and time

---

### 1.2 Harvesting Methods

| Method | Description | Labor | Cost | Losses |
|--------|-------------|-------|------|--------|
| **Manual** | Hand tools (sickle) | High | Low capital | Medium |
| **Semi-mechanized** | Reaper, binder | Medium | Medium | Low |
| **Fully mechanized** | Combine harvester | Low | High capital | Very low |

---

### 1.3 Harvesting Operations

**Sequential Operations:**
1. **Cutting** - Severing crop from field
2. **Gathering** - Collecting cut crop
3. **Threshing** - Separating grain from straw
4. **Cleaning** - Removing chaff and impurities
5. **Handling** - Bagging and transport

**Integration:**
- **Reaper:** Cutting + gathering
- **Binder:** Cutting + gathering + bundling
- **Combine:** All operations in one pass

---

### 1.4 Maturity Indices

**Harvesting Time:**
- **Physiological maturity:** Maximum dry matter
- **Harvest maturity:** Optimum for harvesting

**Moisture Content at Harvest:**
- Wheat: 18-20%
- Rice: 20-25%
- Maize: 20-25%
- Soybean: 13-15%

## 2. Reapers

### 2.1 Definition and Types

**Reaper:** Machine that cuts standing crop and lays it in windrows.

**Types:**

**a) Vertical Conveyor Reaper:**
- Most common type
- Vertical conveyor belt
- Cuts and places in side windrow

**b) Self-propelled Reaper:**
- Engine-powered
- Operator walks behind
- 1.2-1.8 m cutting width

**c) Tractor Mounted/Drawn Reaper:**
- Tractor PTO-driven
- Larger cutting width
- Higher capacity

---

### 2.2 Reaper Components

**Main Parts:**

1. **Cutter bar** - Reciprocating knife
2. **Reel** - Gathers crop to cutter bar
3. **Dividers** - Separates crop to be cut
4. **Platform** - Holds cut crop
5. **Conveyor** - Delivers crop to side
6. **Ground wheel** - Drives mechanism
7. **Engine/PTO** - Power source

---

### 2.3 Cutter Bar

**Types:**

**a) Reciprocating Knife:**
- Knife oscillates horizontally
- Serrated or smooth edge
- Speed: 800-1200 strokes/min

**b) Rotary Cutter:**
- Rotating blades
- High speed (1500-3000 rpm)
- Better for lodged crops

**Components:**
- **Knife/Blade** - Cutting edge
- **Guards** - Support and guide
- **Ledger plates** - Stationary cutting edge
- **Knife head** - Reciprocating mechanism

**Knife Speed:**
$$
V_k = 2 \times L \times N / 60
$$

Where:
- V_k = Knife speed (m/s)
- L = Stroke length (m)
- N = Strokes per minute (spm)

**Typical:** Stroke = 76 mm, Speed = 900 spm
$$
V_k = 2 \times 0.076 \times 900 / 60 = 2.28 \text{ m/s}
$$

---

### 2.4 Reel

**Function:**
- Pushes crop against cutter bar
- Controls crop flow
- Places cut crop on platform

**Types:**
- **Bat reel** - Straight wooden/metal bats
- **Pick-up reel** - Tines pick up lodged crop
- **Plastic finger reel** - Gentle handling

**Reel Index:**
$$
\text{Reel Index} = \frac{\text{Peripheral speed of reel}}{\text{Forward speed}}
$$

**Optimal Range:** 1.2 to 1.5

**Reel Positioning:**
- Height: Just above center of gravity of crop
- Horizontal: 15-30 cm ahead of cutter bar

---

### 2.5 Reaper Specifications

**Standard Specifications:**
- Cutting width: 1.2-1.8 m
- Cutter bar speed: 800-1200 spm
- Reel diameter: 50-80 cm
- Reel speed: 30-50 rpm
- Forward speed: 2-4 km/h
- Stubble height: 10-20 cm
- Field capacity: 0.3-0.5 ha/h
- Power: 3-5 HP (self-propelled)

---

## 3. Combine Harvesters

### 3.1 Definition

**Combine Harvester:** Machine that performs cutting, threshing, separation, and cleaning in one operation.

**Types:**

**a) Self-propelled Combine:**
- Own engine and drive
- High capacity (1-2 ha/h)
- Expensive

**b) Tractor-drawn/PTO Combine:**
- Smaller size
- Lower capacity (0.4-0.8 ha/h)
- Economical

---

### 3.2 Main Systems of Combine

**Major Components:**

1. **Header** - Cutting and gathering
2. **Feeding mechanism** - Feeds crop to threshing
3. **Threshing unit** - Separates grain
4. **Separation unit** - Separates grain from straw
5. **Cleaning unit** - Removes chaff
6. **Grain handling** - Elevators and tank
7. **Straw management** - Chopper and spreader

---

### 3.3 Header (Cutting Platform)

**Components:**
- Cutter bar (reciprocating or rotary)
- Reel (bat or pick-up type)
- Platform auger
- Dividers

**Width:** 3-6 m (small), 6-12 m (large)

**Auger Function:**
- Collects cut crop from platform
- Feeds to feeder house

---

### 3.4 Threshing Unit

**Principle:** Impact, rubbing, and combing action.

**Components:**

**a) Threshing Cylinder (Drum):**
- Rasp bar type (rice, wheat)
- Peg-tooth type (soybean, pulses)
- Diameter: 40-60 cm
- Length: 100-150 cm
- Speed: 500-1200 rpm

**b) Concave:**
- Semicircular grate below cylinder
- Wire mesh or slotted type
- Adjustable clearance: 10-25 mm

**Threshing Cylinder Speed:**

**Linear speed (peripheral velocity):**
$$
V_c = \frac{\pi D N}{60}
$$

Where:
- V_c = Cylinder speed (m/s)
- D = Cylinder diameter (m)
- N = RPM

**Typical Values:**
- Wheat/Rice: 18-25 m/s (700-1000 rpm)
- Pulses: 10-15 m/s (400-600 rpm)

**Concave Clearance:**
- Entry: 25-30 mm
- Exit: 10-15 mm

---

### 3.5 Separation Unit

**Function:** Separate remaining grain from straw after threshing.

**Types:**

**a) Straw Walkers:**
- 3-5 oscillating walkers
- Length: 3-4 m
- Shakes straw, grain falls through
- Efficiency: 95-98%

**b) Rotary Separator:**
- Rotating cage with helical fins
- Better separation
- Less grain damage
- Used in modern combines

---

### 3.6 Cleaning Unit

**Function:** Remove chaff, dust, and broken straw from grain.

**Components:**

**a) Oscillating Sieves (Chaffer and Sieve):**
- **Upper sieve (Chaffer):** Larger openings (12-20 mm)
- **Lower sieve (Grain pan):** Smaller openings (6-12 mm)
- Oscillation: 180-250 strokes/min

**b) Blower (Fan):**
- Provides air stream
- Blows away light material
- Speed: 600-900 rpm
- Air velocity: 6-12 m/s

**Adjustment:**
- Sieve opening: Varies with crop
- Fan speed: Varies with moisture and crop
- Too much air: Grain loss
- Too little air: Impure grain

---

### 3.7 Grain Handling System

**Components:**

1. **Augers** - Horizontal conveyance
2. **Elevators** - Vertical conveyance (bucket or paddle)
3. **Grain tank** - Temporary storage (500-3000 liters)
4. **Unloading auger** - Transfers to trailer

---

### 3.8 Combine Harvester Specifications

**Small Combine (PTO-driven):**
- Cutting width: 2-3 m
- Threshing width: 60-90 cm
- Cylinder diameter: 40-50 cm
- Cylinder speed: 600-1000 rpm
- Field capacity: 0.4-0.8 ha/h
- Tractor power: 40-60 HP

**Large Self-propelled:**
- Cutting width: 4-8 m
- Threshing width: 120-150 cm
- Cylinder diameter: 50-60 cm
- Engine power: 100-200 HP
- Field capacity: 1-3 ha/h
- Grain tank: 2000-4000 liters

## 4. Threshers

### 4.1 Definition and Types

**Thresher:** Machine that separates grain from the harvested crop.

**Types by Power:**
- **Manual thresher** - Pedal operated
- **Power thresher** - Engine/motor driven

**Types by Operation:**
- **Batch type** - Feed in batches
- **Continuous flow** - Continuous feeding

---

### 4.2 Thresher Components

**Main Parts:**

1. **Threshing cylinder** - Rotating drum with pegs/rasp bars
2. **Concave** - Grate below cylinder
3. **Blower** - Cleaning fan
4. **Oscillating sieves** - Separates grain from chaff
5. **Feeding chute** - Feeds crop
6. **Frame and power transmission** - Structure and drives

---

### 4.3 Threshing Mechanisms

**Methods:**

**a) Impact:**
- High-speed rotating cylinder hits grain
- Rasp bar thresher

**b) Rubbing:**
- Crop rubbed between surfaces
- Stone roller thresher

**c) Combination:**
- Impact + rubbing
- Most common in modern threshers

---

### 4.4 Threshing Cylinder Design

**Types of Teeth:**

**a) Rasp Bar:**
- For wheat, rice, barley
- Blunt rectangular bars
- Less grain damage

**b) Peg Tooth:**
- For pulses, soybean
- Pointed pegs
- Better threshing action

**c) Wire Loop:**
- For delicate crops
- Gentle threshing

**Cylinder Specifications:**
- Diameter: 40-60 cm
- Length: 60-100 cm
- Speed: 400-1200 rpm (crop dependent)
- Number of bars/pegs: 6-12

**Speed Calculation:**
$$
N = \frac{60 \times V_c}{\pi D}
$$

Where:
- N = RPM
- V_c = Desired peripheral speed (m/s)
- D = Diameter (m)

---

### 4.5 Concave

**Function:** 
- Provides resistance for threshing
- Allows grain to pass through
- Retains straw

**Types:**
- **Open grate** - Rods with spacing
- **Perforated sheet** - Holes in metal sheet
- **Wire mesh** - Woven wire

**Clearance:**
- Front: 25-35 mm
- Rear: 10-20 mm
- Adjustable for different crops

**Wraparound Angle:**
- 90Â° to 120Â° for most crops
- More angle = more threshing, more damage

---

### 4.6 Thresher Specifications

**Standard Specifications:**
- Cylinder diameter: 40-60 cm
- Cylinder length: 60-100 cm
- Cylinder speed: 600-1000 rpm (wheat/rice)
- Concave clearance: 10-30 mm
- Blower speed: 600-900 rpm
- Feed rate: 300-600 kg/h
- Power: 5-15 HP
- Capacity: 200-500 kg/h (grain output)

## 5. Winnowers and Cleaners

### 5.1 Winnower

**Function:** Separate grain from chaff using air stream.

**Principle:** 
- Grain is heavier, falls down
- Chaff is lighter, blown away

**Components:**
- Hopper
- Oscillating sieve
- Fan (centrifugal)
- Adjustable shutters

**Specifications:**
- Capacity: 300-1000 kg/h
- Power: 1-3 HP
- Fan speed: 600-1200 rpm

---

### 5.2 Grain Cleaner

**Function:** Remove impurities and grade grain.

**Types:**

**a) Air Screen Cleaner:**
- Air blast + sieves
- Multiple sieve decks
- High purity (98-99%)

**b) Indent Cylinder:**
- For length separation
- Rotates and lifts by indents
- Separates broken grain

**c) Gravity Separator:**
- Based on density
- Oscillating deck with air
- Separates damaged/lightweight grain

---

## 6. Maize and Groundnut Harvesters

### 6.1 Maize Harvester

**Types:**

**a) Maize Picker:**
- Removes ears from stalk
- Leaves stalk standing

**b) Maize Combine:**
- Picks ears
- Shells corn from cob
- Cleans and stores grain

**Components:**
- Snapping rolls (for ear picking)
- Husking bed (removes husk)
- Shelling unit (if combine type)

---

### 6.2 Groundnut Harvester

**Types:**

**a) Digger:**
- Digs up plants
- Inverts and windrows

**b) Digger-Shaker-Windrower:**
- Digs, shakes soil off
- Places in windrow

**c) Combine Harvester:**
- Digs, separates pods
- Cleans and collects

**Components:**
- Blade/digger
- Shaking mechanism
- Separating unit
- Cleaning fan

## 7. Crop Losses

### 7.1 Types of Losses

**Pre-harvest Losses:**
- Shattering
- Bird damage
- Lodging
- Weather damage

**Harvest Losses:**

**a) Gathering Loss:**
- Uncut crop
- Dropped heads
- Shattered grain

**b) Threshing Loss:**
- Unthreshed grain (with straw)
- Damaged grain

**c) Separation Loss:**
- Grain with straw/chaff
- Walker loss

**d) Cleaning Loss:**
- Over blowing
- Grain blown away

---

### 7.2 Loss Measurement

**Total Loss:**
$$
\text{Total Loss (\%)} = \frac{\text{Weight of grain lost}}{\text{Total grain yield}} \times 100
$$

**Acceptable Limits:**
- **Total combine loss:** < 2% (for cereals)
- **Thresher loss:** < 1%
- **Gathering loss:** < 0.5%
- **Cleaning loss:** < 0.5%

**Measurement Procedure:**
1. Collect lost grain from known area
2. Weigh the grain
3. Calculate percentage

**Example:**
If 0.2 kg grain lost in 10 mÂ² area, and yield is 40 q/ha:

Area = 10 mÂ² = 0.001 ha

Yield = 40 q/ha = 4000 kg/ha

Grain lost/ha = 0.2 / 0.001 = 200 kg/ha

Loss % = (200 / 4000) Ã— 100 = 5%

---

### 7.3 Factors Affecting Losses

**Crop Factors:**
- Moisture content
- Lodging
- Maturity stage
- Variety (shattering tendency)

**Machine Factors:**
- Forward speed
- Cylinder speed
- Concave clearance
- Reel speed and position
- Fan speed
- Sieve opening

**Operator Factors:**
- Adjustments
- Maintenance
- Operating skills

## 8. Performance Parameters

### 8.1 Field Capacity

**Theoretical Field Capacity:**
$$
\text{TFC (ha/h)} = \frac{W \times S}{10}
$$

**Effective Field Capacity:**
$$
\text{EFC (ha/h)} = \frac{\text{Area harvested (ha)}}{\text{Total time (h)}}
$$

**Field Efficiency:**
$$
\eta_f = \frac{\text{EFC}}{\text{TFC}} \times 100\%
$$

**Typical Efficiencies:**
- Reaper: 65-75%
- Small combine: 60-70%
- Large combine: 70-80%

---

### 8.2 Threshing Efficiency

**Threshing Efficiency:**
$$
\eta_t = \frac{\text{Grain threshed}}{\text{Total grain in crop}} \times 100\%
$$

**Should be:** > 99% for good threshing

**Cleaning Efficiency:**
$$
\eta_c = \frac{\text{Clean grain collected}}{\text{Total grain threshed}} \times 100\%
$$

**Should be:** > 98% for good cleaning

---

### 8.3 Grain Damage

**Damage Percentage:**
$$
\text{Damage (\%)} = \frac{\text{Damaged grains}}{\text{Total grains}} \times 100
$$

**Acceptable:** < 1% for food grains

**Types of Damage:**
- Broken grains
- Cracked grains
- Skinned grains

**Causes:**
- High cylinder speed
- Low moisture content
- Excessive impact

---

### 8.4 Feed Rate

**Feed Rate:**
$$
Q_f = \frac{\text{Material fed (kg)}}{\text{Time (h)}}
$$

Unit: kg/h

**For Combine:**
$$
Q_f = \frac{Y \times W \times S \times 100}{10}
$$

Where:
- Y = Crop yield (kg/ha)
- W = Cutting width (m)
- S = Forward speed (km/h)

---

### 8.5 Capacity

**Output Capacity:**
$$
C_o = \frac{\text{Grain output (kg)}}{\text{Time (h)}}
$$

**Area Capacity:**
$$
C_a = \frac{\text{Area covered (ha)}}{\text{Time (h)}}
$$

---

### 8.6 Power Requirement

**Power for Threshing:**
$$
P_t = \frac{T \times 2\pi N}{60000}
$$

Where:
- P_t = Power (kW)
- T = Torque (Nm)
- N = RPM

**Specific Energy:**
$$
E_s = \frac{\text{Energy (kJ)}}{\text{Mass threshed (kg)}}
$$

Typical: 8-15 kJ/kg for cereals
